Marie Curie

Marie Curie (born 1867) was a Polish physicist and chemist who lived and worked in France. She and her husband - Pierre Curie - discovered and defined radioactivity and discovered the radioactive atoms Polonium and Radium.
Marie Curie received two Nobel Prizes. 1903 in Physics and 1911 in Chemistry. She was the first women to ever receive this prestigious price and is yet the only women ever to receive 2 Nobel Prizes.
She died in 1934. It is believed her death was due to long-term exposure to radiation.
