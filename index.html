Marie Curie

Marrie Curie was a Polish physicist and chemist, born in 1867, who lived and worked in France. She and her husband Pierre Curie discovered and defined radioactivity along with the radioactive atoms Polonium and Radium. Marie Curie received a nobel price 1903 in Physics and 1911 in Chemestry. She was the first women to ever receive this presticous price and is yet the only women ever to receive 2 nobel prices. She died in 1934. It is believed her death was due to long-term exposure to radiation.
